1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

All projects start with good intentions. However, knowing your team capabilities and abilities is imperative. Overextending and under-delivering is a common mistake. Careful planning and working out a good time frame will help in the long run. Breaking up the project into small manageable chunks helps keep a good work cadence and help meets deadlines. A good structure of the whole process should be well documented and easy to comprehend. Every company has a unique working environment. But good industry standard working practises should be applied whenever possible

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

Spending time to research the latest software packages and industry trends will help guide the initial phase of any project or process. A good group consensus from colleges is also a prerequisite. Acquired knowledge may prove a deciding factor in finalizing any framework. Good budgeting and sourcing reputable technology company vendors are critical. Buying out of date or overpriced kit may prove to be a costly error. If your project requires creative software then Apple and adobe products might be the right choice if the company finances could afford such technology. If the tasks require only basic IT systems then used parts or short term hire might be a variable solution.

Trello is a fantastic visual aid to help plan and schedule a project, from the planning stage to the final build or completion. Sharing Google documents helps enormously in coordinating tasks even with groups that are not local to each other. GitHub provides awesome code version with its suite of lightweight tools to enable a smooth build process between partners.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

To comply with the GDPR law all personal data has to be vetted before adding to any digital projects. Careful planning and robust secure practises must be followed before any project goes beyond the initial planning stage. In larger companies, a dedicated Data Protection Officer must oversee the entire operation.
